The Windy Giant

Neptune is the eighth and most distant planet from the Sun, an ice giant with the strongest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds up to 2,100 km/h. Named after the Roman god of the sea, Neptune has a striking deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere. It was the first planet to be discovered through mathematical predictions rather than direct observation, discovered in 1846. Neptune has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest.
